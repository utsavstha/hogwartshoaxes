Muggle Medics: Are They Finally Catching Up? Alarming Progress in Non-Magical Healing.

April 2, 2025 | By Barnaby Bumble

In what can only be described as a development that has the Ministry of Magic’s Department of International Magical Cooperation scratching their heads, Muggles appear to be making significant strides in their so-called ‘medical technology.’ Whispers from Obliviators assigned to Muggle hospitals (mostly to clean up after overzealous Healers use too much Skelegro on unsuspecting patients, honestly) suggest these non-magical folk are developing methods to address ailments that, until recently, were exclusively the domain of potions and charms.

Take, for instance, their ‘surgery.’ Apparently, these Muggles, lacking even the most basic Severing Charm, are resorting to…cutting people open. With metal instruments! Madam Pomfrey, when informed of this practice, nearly choked on her Bertie Bott’s Every Flavour Bean. “Cutting? Open? Where’s the dignity? One flicks a wand, applies a Dittany poultice, and it’s all done!” she exclaimed, before adding, “Though I do admit, Dittany prices have been outrageous lately. Perhaps I should invest in a Muggle scalpel… strictly for research purposes, of course.”

Furthermore, sources indicate Muggles have developed something called ‘vaccinations.’ These apparently offer ‘immunity’ from diseases. One can’t help but wonder if this is just a particularly cleverly disguised version of a poorly brewed Strengthening Solution. Professor Slughorn, our resident Potions Master, remained skeptical. “Immunity, eh? Without a single newt spleen or Bezoar in sight? Preposterous! Though,” he added thoughtfully, stroking his walrus mustache, “perhaps I could incorporate this ‘vaccination’ principle into a new line of tonics. Think of the marketing possibilities! ‘Slughorn’s Immunity Elixir - No Newt Spleens, Guaranteed!’”

Perhaps the most unsettling development is the Muggle obsession with ‘imaging technology.’ They can, allegedly, ‘see inside’ the human body without the need for a Scrying Charm or even a simple Revealing Spell. This has led to widespread paranoia among certain members of the Wizengamot, with some fearing their illicit stash of Fudge Flies might be discovered during a routine Muggle ‘scan.’

While the magical community remains cautiously optimistic (and slightly terrified) about these advancements, one thing is clear: we cannot afford to be complacent. The Ministry is forming a special task force, led by Arthur Weasley (who, to everyone’s surprise, volunteered), to study Muggle medical techniques and determine whether they pose a threat, a benefit, or merely a source of endless amusement. One thing’s for sure: the next few years promise to be… interesting. And hopefully, less messy than a botched Babbling Beverage experiment.
