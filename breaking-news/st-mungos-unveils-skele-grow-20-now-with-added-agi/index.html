St. Mungo's Unveils 'Skele-Grow 2.0': Now With Added Agility and a Reduced 'Wobbly Leg' Guarantee!

March 30, 2025 | By Elara Thistlewick

St. Mungo’s Hospital for Magical Maladies and Injuries announced today a breakthrough in skeletal regeneration. Lead Healer Smethwyck, sporting a rather suspiciously straight spine himself, revealed the new and improved Skele-Grow potion, dubbed ‘Skele-Grow 2.0’.

‘For years,’ Healer Smethwyck began, wiping a bead of sweat from his brow (perhaps a side effect of original Skele-Grow?), ‘patients have complained of the dreaded ‘wobbly leg’ phenomenon post-regeneration. Not to mention the… shall we say… rigidity some experienced. Skele-Grow 2.0 addresses these concerns directly!’

The potion, now infused with ethically-sourced Unicorn tail hair (the subject of a heated debate at last week’s Unicorn Welfare Symposium), promises increased bone density and flexibility. Early trials on volunteer Skeletal Charms students have shown remarkable results. One student, initially diagnosed with a Severely-Disintegrated-Ulna-and-Radius condition after a particularly enthusiastic attempt at a Levitation Charm, was reportedly back on his Quidditch broom within a week – performing loop-the-loops and Barrel Rolls, with only a minor case of post-flight nausea.

However, the Ministry of Magic’s Department of Magical Law Enforcement has issued a cautionary statement. ‘While we applaud the advancements in healing,’ stated a Ministry spokesperson (presumably after consulting with a lawyer), ‘we strongly advise against using Skele-Grow 2.0 to artificially enhance athletic performance or, Merlin forbid, to ‘grow’ extra limbs. Such misuse will be met with the full force of the law, and potentially a very confused and unbalanced body.’

Rumors persist that Draco Malfoy, reportedly suffering lingering effects from a rather unfortunate incident involving a Bludger and a particularly aggressive Hippogriff, is already on the waiting list. Healer Smethwyck declined to comment on specific patient cases, but did add, with a knowing wink, ‘Let’s just say, some bones are more… stubbornly Malfoy-able than others.’
